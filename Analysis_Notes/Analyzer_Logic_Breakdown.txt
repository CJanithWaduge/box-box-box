======================================================================
TECHNICAL DOCUMENTATION: ANALYZER.PY LOGIC AND DATA INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

1. OBJECTIVE
------------
The 'analyzer.py' script was developed as a data-mining utility to 
process the historical dataset containing 30,000 race records. The 
primary intent is the filtration of data into specific scenarios based 
on (Track + Temperature) to identify deterministic patterns.

2. OPERATIONAL LOGIC
--------------------
- File Processing: Systematic scanning of the 'data/historical_races/' 
  directory for JSON batch files is performed.
- Grouping Mechanism: Data is organized into a dictionary structure 
  where unique combinations of Track and Temperature serve as keys.
- Attribute Extraction: For every driver entry, the Driver ID, 
  Finishing Position (Rank), Starting Tire, and Pit-Stop count 
  are extracted and mapped.

3. DATA INTERPRETATION (CASE STUDY: SUZUKA @ 27°C)
--------------------------------------------------
The extracted output for Suzuka at 27°C with a Base Lap Time of 84.5s 
provides the following insights:

- Significance of Base Lap Time: 
  The value of 84.5s is established as the fundamental track speed. 
  It serves as the mathematical reference point; all tire-related 
  time penalties or advantages are calculated relative to this value.

- Comparative Analysis of Identical Starting Compounds (MEDIUM):
  A discrepancy in finishing positions between D001 (P1) and D002 (P4) 
  is observed despite identical starting tires.
  * D001 (P1): Achieved via a 1-pit stop strategy, indicating 
    maximum time efficiency.
  * D002 (P4): Resulted from a 2-pit stop strategy. The additional 
    22-second pit lane penalty was not offset by the gain in tire speed.

- Assessment of SOFT Compound Performance:
  D003 (starting on SOFT tires) concluded in P13. Under the 27°C 
  thermal conditions, a high rate of degradation is inferred, 
  rendering the SOFT compound less effective over the race distance.

4. MATHEMATICAL OBJECTIVES
--------------------------
The grouped data is utilized to derive the following variables:
- Tire Offset (Oc): The constant time difference between compounds.
- Wear Rate (Wc): The linear increase in lap time per lap aged.
- Pit Penalty: Verification of the deterministic 22s pit lane duration.

5. CONCLUSION
-------------
The analysis confirms that the simulation operates on deterministic 
calculations rather than stochastic variables. The relationship 
between Base Time, Tire Deltas, and Wear is now clearly evidenced 
for the development of the final prediction model.
